I work with paper

 
 
I‘m a technical communicator with experience in the electrical engineering, software development, and lab management fields. I’ve been fortunate to have discovered my interest in technical writing after my exposure to the electrical engineering, mathematics, and software development. I’ve spent time 5 years in the engineering research community developing laboratory safety manuals for four electrical engineering laboratories, as well as other written management solutions. During this period I joined a local software startup in Lubbock, TX, where I spent time as a front-end developer on a freelance app concept. Although, the concept never took off the ground and a final product was never shipped; I gained invaluable experience with markup languages, object-oriented programming, product design from ideation to minimum-viable-product (MVP), as well as version control and Agile work practices. Most recently, I worked as a full-time staff member in a semiconductor testing instruction program at Texas Tech University. There, I managed equipment, information, and access for the laboratory and I created a lab management system to coordinate lab and equipment availability and access for undergraduate, graduate, staff, and cross-disciplinary research and education collaborations.
